# Supply Chain Performance, Cost Optimization & Supplier Intelligence Analysis

## Project Overview

Modern supply chains generate enormous volumes of operational data across procurement, inventory management, logistics, and supplier relationships. However, operational inefficiencies often remain hidden until they begin affecting profitability, customer satisfaction, or inventory performance.

Organizations frequently monitor revenue growth without understanding whether increasing sales are being supported by efficient supply chain operations. High-performing products may simultaneously suffer from excessive shipping costs, elevated defect rates, or supplier-related delays that quietly erode profitability.

This project provides a comprehensive supply chain performance analysis designed to uncover operational inefficiencies, identify cost optimization opportunities, and evaluate supplier performance across multiple dimensions of the supply chain.

The objective is not simply to report revenue figures but to answer critical business questions such as:

> - Which product categories contribute most to business performance?
> - Where are operational costs increasing unnecessarily?
> - Which suppliers are negatively affecting supply chain efficiency?
> - How do shipping carriers impact logistics costs?
> - Which products exhibit elevated defect rates?
> - What opportunities exist to improve operational performance and profitability?

## Key Insights

Several important operational trends emerged from the analysis.

### Revenue Performance

> - Skincare products generate the highest revenue across the portfolio.

This finding highlights strong market demand while simultaneously emphasizing the importance of maintaining inventory availability and supplier performance within this category.

### Logistics Performance

The analysis identified meaningful differences in:

> - Shipping Costs
> - Carrier Performance
> - Supplier Efficiency

These variations present opportunities for operational improvements capable of reducing supply chain costs without negatively affecting customer experience.

### Quality Performance

Product defect rates vary across suppliers and product categories, highlighting opportunities for improving:

- Quality Control Processes
- Supplier Selection Strategies
- Inventory Planning

---

## Business Recommendations

Based on the findings, several operational improvement opportunities were identified.

### Cost Optimization

- Evaluate alternative logistics providers where shipping costs materially exceed portfolio averages.
- Continuously monitor carrier performance across product categories.

### Supplier Performance Management

- Renegotiate lead times with underperforming suppliers.
- Establish supplier performance scorecards for ongoing monitoring.

### Quality Improvements

- Strengthen quality assurance procedures across product categories exhibiting elevated defect rates.
- Monitor defect trends proactively to reduce operational losses and improve customer satisfaction.

### Inventory Optimization

- Prioritize inventory planning for high-performing product categories to minimize stock-related disruptions.
